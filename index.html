Cannabis in Maryland, 2023

A Manyfold Path

Creating a successful market will mean satisfying a lot of concerns. Lawmakwers had to consider everything from public safety to atoning for some of the War on Drugs’ injustices, to zoning, to tax revenue, and everything in between, all against the backdrop of an industry-wide oversupply crisis that has dragged prices down, forced businesses into crisis and called into question the viability of other states’ legalization models.

“All we know is what they didn’t do right.”
- House Economic Matters Committee Chairman Wayne Newton

Such a complex issue has resulted in a complex law. The version of the bill that passed through Maryland’s state legislature is over 100 pages of dense legal text that lays out each aspect of legalization in considerable detail and with considerable care. But as Marylanders wonder what the functioning recreational market will look like in practice, not many have the time to pore over a 100-page document.

That’s where we come in.

We spent the last few weeks poring over Maryland's new cannabis law, talking to the lawmakers who crafted it, and speaking with industry experts to try to figure out how buying and selling cannabis will change in Maryland starting July 1st.
